🔴 Critical fixes — do these first
Security
Full emails exposed in JS source
All fullEmail values (e.g. nicole.clausen@dhl.com, lina.wehr@basf.com) are plaintext in the JS data object. Anyone can open DevTools and copy them without paying. Move the full emails server-side or behind an API that checks payment status before revealing them.
Conversion
No lead capture form — only WhatsApp CTAs
All 3 pricing cards send the visitor to WhatsApp. If someone isn't on mobile or doesn't want to open WhatsApp, they leave. Add an email opt-in or a simple embedded form (Tally, Typeform, or even a mailto form) as a fallback path. This also means you collect zero leads passively.
Trust
Fake live-visitor counter
The "مباشر: -- طالب يتصفحون الآن" badge uses a random number generator, not real data. Sophisticated visitors will notice this (especially if they reload and see a wildly different number), which destroys trust for a service where trust is everything. Either remove it or replace it with a real counter (e.g. via a simple Supabase realtime query).
Legal
No privacy policy, no terms, no refund policy
You're collecting WhatsApp contacts and selling a paid service. Moroccan and EU visitors both expect (and legally in some cases require) a privacy statement. Without it, many users won't pay. Add a simple footer with a 3-line privacy note and a refund/guarantee policy — it's also a strong conversion booster.
🟡 High-impact improvements
SEO
Zero SEO meta tags
No <meta description>, no Open Graph tags (og:title, og:image), no canonical URL. If someone shares the link on WhatsApp or Facebook it shows a blank preview. Add at minimum: description, og:title, og:description, og:image (a 1200×630 branded image), and og:url.
Analytics
No analytics or event tracking
You have no way of knowing how many people visit, which package they look at most, or where they drop off. Add Google Analytics 4 (free) or Plausible (privacy-friendly, €9/mo). At minimum, add a click event on each WhatsApp CTA so you know your conversion rate per package.
Conversion
No urgency mechanism that's real
The "🔥 الأكثر طلباً" badge is good but static. Consider adding a real scarcity element — e.g. "نقبل 5 طلبات فقط هذا الأسبوع" (only 5 slots this week) with an actual counter stored server-side, or a countdown timer to a weekly deadline. Real scarcity converts better than fake social proof.
Trust
WhatsApp testimonials look fabricated
The WA-style chat bubbles are a clever UI pattern but anonymous. Add at minimum: a first name + city, a date, and ideally a profile photo (even blurred/initials). Bonus: link to a real Google review or a TikTok/Reels success story. Even one real video testimonial crushes 20 text bubbles.
UX
Two near-identical HTML files (index + landing)
Both files share 99% of the same code and data. This is a maintenance nightmare — every update must be made twice, and they can drift out of sync. Consolidate into one file, or use a simple static site generator (Astro, 11ty) or even a shared JS import to share the data/components.
🟢 Tools & features to add
Tool
AI-powered Ausbildung match quiz high impact
Add a 5-question quiz: "What's your education level? What city do you prefer? What field?". Output a personalised package recommendation + tailored WhatsApp message. This increases time-on-site and filters serious leads. Can be built as a Claude-powered artifact in 1 session.
Tool
Free Anschreiben (cover letter) generator
A simple AI-powered form: user enters name, field of interest, and language level → generates a ready-to-use German cover letter. This is a massive lead magnet. Collect their WhatsApp/email before showing the result. Builds trust and filters qualified leads from tyre-kickers.
Tool
Progress tracker for paying clients
A private page (or WhatsApp-linked) where clients can see "Applications sent: 47 / 200 · Opened: 12 · Replies: 2". Even a static version you update manually builds enormous trust and reduces "where is my file?" WhatsApp messages from clients.
Content
Blog / FAQ section in Arabic
Articles like "كيف تكتب CV ألماني من المغرب" (how to write a German CV from Morocco) drive organic search traffic from Google. Your audience is actively searching these questions. Even 5 well-written articles can bring consistent free traffic over time.
UX
Sticky WhatsApp floating button
On mobile, the CTA buttons inside package cards are far down the page. Add a persistent floating WhatsApp button (bottom-right, outside the normal scroll flow) so the path to contact is always one tap away, regardless of where the user is on the page.
